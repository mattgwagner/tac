# April TLP Training - Planning Notes

## Purpose

Phase 2 TLP-focused training to prepare 24 Officer Candidates for Phase 3. This is the primary TLP training window — May has limited availability due to the graduation run and staff ride consuming most of the available time.

## Available Time

**April:** Thursday afternoon (first formation 1300) + Friday all day. Civil twilight at 1953 both days. Candidates must be back Friday evening — cleaned up, weapons turned in — for a PHA medical exam Saturday morning.

**May:** Some time available but significantly constrained by graduation run and staff ride (full-day event). Do not count on May for meaningful TLP repetitions.

## Candidate Assessment (from FLX AAR)

24 candidates, roughly split into two groups based on self-assessment:

- **~12 combat arms / high-speed:** Self-identified as ready to ramp up complexity. Want more tactical challenge, not just more reps. Based on AAR feedback.
- **~12 fundamentals / more reps:** Indicated they need more pure repetitions at conducting TLPs for STX lanes. Want to focus on the planning process itself.

This split drives the two-track training concept used in both COAs.

## Two-Track Training Concept

Rather than forcing all 24 through identical training, run two concurrent tracks from the same AO:

**Combat Arms (CA) Track (~12 candidates, 2 tactical TACs):**
- End-to-end mission execution every lane: receive OPORD → TLPs → rehearsal → execute with blanks → AAR
- Each lane ~3-4 hours
- Every candidate leads at least once
- Blank fire on all iterations
- Thursday evening limited visibility lane if time permits
- OPFOR from non-leading candidates or cross-leveled from FND track

**Fundamentals (FND) Track (~12 candidates, 2 TACs + floating instructor):**
- All 3 squads (4 candidates each) plan simultaneously off parallel OPORDs
- Each planning cycle ~2-2.5 hours
- Rotate squad leaders every iteration — everyone leads at least once
- Execute approximately every other plan with abbreviated execution (leader's recon, walk-through at patrol speed)
- Candidates who never connect plans to ground truth develop a disconnect — some execution is important even if focus is planning
- Dry-fire acceptable for planning-only reps; blanks for execution iterations

Both tracks share: patrol base overnight Thursday, consolidated AAR Friday afternoon, same AO for mutual cadre/logistics support.

### COA 2: S9/S10 Training Areas

**Fallback if blanks not approved for LTA, or if Commander prefers new environment.**

- Candidates ruck 2.5-3 miles to S9/S10 Thursday afternoon (90 min in April heat)
- ~13.5 hours of usable TLP training time (lost ~2.5 hrs to movement)
- CA track: 3-4 full lanes

- S9/S10 not available in May — this is the only window if we want to use that terrain

**If selected, recommend:** Van candidates there (cut the ruck) and van them back. The ruck costs training time and elevates heat risk without advancing the primary TLP objective.

## Heat Considerations

April at Camp Blanding. This is a real constraint, not a footnote.

- Daytime highs 80-90F, high humidity
- Peak heat 1300-1700 — which is exactly when Thursday training starts
- Enforce 1-quart-per-hour minimum
- Build shade breaks into FND track planning cycles (candidates will be heads-down over maps in the sun)
- CA track candidates will be moving, which helps airflow but increases exertion
- Cadre must actively monitor — don't rely on candidates to self-report
- Water resupply plan via GSA vans is critical for both COAs
- LTA (COA 1) is ~5 min from garrison medical; S9/S10 (COA 2) is ~15-20 min

## Overnight Patrol Base

Common to both COAs. Training value includes:
- Security plan (sector sketches, observation posts, LP/OP)
- Sleep plan (rotation schedule, minimum 4 hours per candidate)
- Stand-to (0530-0600)
- Priorities of work
- Continuous operations feel — bridges Thursday training into Friday without a reset

The patrol base itself is a trained task (STX 008 exists in the library). Can be evaluated or treated as a training event in its own right.
