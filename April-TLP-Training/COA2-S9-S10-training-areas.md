# COA 2: April TLP Training - S9/S10 Training Areas

## Classification: UNCLASSIFIED // FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY

## Overview

**Training Event:** Phase 2 TLP Focused Training (Pre-Phase 3 Preparation)
**Dates:** Thursday-Friday, April 2026 (specific drill weekend TBD)
**Location:** S9 and S10 Training Areas, Camp Blanding Joint Training Center, FL (2.5-3 miles from company area)
**Duration:** ~28 hours continuous field operations (Thursday 1330 - Friday 1800)
**Candidates:** 24 Officer Candidates
**Cadre Required:** 4 TACs + 1 Instructor (5 total)

## Concept of the Operation

Conduct differentiated TLP-focused training from S9/S10 training areas using two concurrent training tracks: a Combat Arms (CA) track emphasizing end-to-end mission execution with blank fire, and a Fundamentals (FND) track emphasizing maximum TLP planning repetitions. Candidates conduct a tactical road march (ruck) to S9/S10 Thursday afternoon as an additional training event. Both tracks operate from the same general area for mutual support. Training includes an overnight patrol base. GSA vans transport equipment and provide logistics support throughout. Candidates return via van or ruck Friday afternoon with sufficient time for weapons cleaning and preparation for Saturday PHA.

### Key Difference from COA 1

This COA is the fallback if blank fire is not approved for the LTA, or if the Commander prefers the training value of a new environment and tactical movement. It trades approximately 1.5-2 hours of TLP training time for the benefits of unfamiliar terrain and a tactical ruck march. It also requires new grid work for all objectives and control measures, and more complex logistics support.

## Training Tracks

### Combat Arms Track (~12 Candidates)

**Cadre:** 2 Tactical/Combat Arms TACs
**Focus:** End-to-end mission execution (receive OPORD, conduct TLPs, rehearse, execute with blank fire, AAR)
**Intent:** Same as COA 1 - challenge candidates who self-identified as ready for increased complexity. New terrain adds an additional layer of challenge.

- Each lane is approximately 3-4 hours (planning through AAR)
- Estimated 3-4 full lane rotations across both training days (reduced from COA 1 due to movement time)
- Each candidate leads at minimum 1 lane as SL/TL
- Blank ammunition used on all execution iterations
- Thursday evening limited visibility lane if time permits (dependent on arrival time at S9/S10)

**Mission Types (drawn from existing STX OPORD library, re-gridded for S9/S10):**
- Ambush (STX 001)
- Movement to Contact (STX 002)
- Deliberate Attack (STX 014/016)
- Raid (STX 003)
- Area Reconnaissance (STX 005)

### Fundamentals Track (~12 Candidates)

**Cadre:** 2 TACs + floating Instructor
**Focus:** Maximum TLP planning repetitions with all three squads planning simultaneously
**Intent:** Same as COA 1 - address candidate-identified need for more planning reps. New terrain provides unfamiliar map work which has its own training value for terrain analysis.

- 3 squads of 4 candidates, all conducting TLPs concurrently
- Each planning cycle runs approximately 2-2.5 hours
- Estimated 5 planning iterations per squad across both training days (reduced from COA 1)
- Rotate squad leaders each iteration
- Execute approximately every other plan (abbreviated)
- Unfamiliar terrain forces more rigorous map-to-ground correlation during execution

**Mission Types:** Same library as CA track, re-gridded for S9/S10.

## Logistics

### Transportation
- **GSA Van 1:** Transport equipment to S9/S10 Thursday 1330; remain at training area as emergency/resupply vehicle; return candidates Friday PM
- **GSA Van 2:** Resupply runs (water, chow) Thursday evening and Friday morning; cadre rotation support; return candidates and equipment Friday PM

### Ammunition
- Same allocation plan as COA 1
- Ammo transported to S9/S10 via GSA van (not carried on ruck)
- Ammo accountability more complex at remote site; designate one TAC as ammo NCO equivalent

### Water/Heat Plan
- Same heat conditions as COA 1, **compounded by Thursday ruck in afternoon heat**
- Pre-position water at S9/S10 via GSA van before candidates arrive
- Mandatory water resupply Thursday evening, Friday morning, and Friday midday
- Ruck march hydration: enforce water consumption at halt points every 15-20 min
- **Higher heat casualty risk than COA 1** due to ruck movement in peak heat with full kit
- S9/S10 distance from garrison medical: ~15-20 min by vehicle (vs. ~5 min from LTA)

### Equipment
- Same as COA 1, PLUS:
- **New OPORD shells required** - all existing STX OPORDs use LTA grids; S9/S10 requires new grid references for all objectives, phase lines, checkpoints, and control measures
- Cadre must conduct a leader's recon of S9/S10 prior to training to identify suitable objective areas, patrol base sites, and lane terrain
- Map boards, protractors, markers transported via GSA van

### Chow
- Thursday: Candidates eat prior to 1300 formation; evening chow via GSA van resupply to S9/S10
- Friday: Morning chow via GSA van to S9/S10; MREs for lunch; candidates eat dinner after return to company area

## Preparation Requirements (Not Required for COA 1)

1. **Leader's recon of S9/S10** - Cadre must walk the terrain to identify objective areas, patrol base sites, and suitable maneuver space for both tracks. Recommend 1-2 TACs conduct recon NLT 1 week prior.
2. **New grid references** - All STX OPORD shells must be re-gridded for S9/S10 terrain. This is significant planning effort.
3. **Route recon for ruck march** - Identify road march route, rest halt locations, and water points for 2.5-3 mile movement.
4. **GSA van staging plan** - Coordinate equipment load plan, pre-positioning timeline, and resupply schedule.

## Advantages

1. **New environment.** Unfamiliar terrain forces more rigorous terrain analysis, map reading, and ground-truth correlation. Candidates cannot rely on familiarity from FLX training.
2. **Tactical road march as additional training.** The ruck to S9/S10 is a realistic tactical movement that trains route planning, movement formations, and march discipline.
3. **Blank fire confirmed.** S9/S10 are standard live/blank fire training areas with no approval ambiguity.
4. **Expanded maneuver space.** S9/S10 may offer more varied terrain and larger maneuver areas than the LTA, enabling more complex CA track scenarios.

## Disadvantages

1. **Lost training time.** Ruck march costs ~90 min Thursday afternoon; return movement costs ~30-60 min Friday afternoon (even by van). Net loss of approximately 2-2.5 hours of TLP training time. CA track drops from 4-5 lanes to 3-4. FND track drops from 6+ planning cycles to ~5.
2. **Significant cadre preparation required.** Leader's recon, new grid references for all OPORDs, route planning, and logistics coordination all require cadre time 1-2 weeks before drill. COA 1 reuses existing LTA grids and OPORD shells with minimal modification.
3. **Higher heat casualty risk.** Thursday afternoon ruck in April Florida heat with full kit, followed by immediate training, elevates heat risk above COA 1 baseline.
4. **More complex logistics.** Resupply, cadre rotation, and emergency response all require GSA van coordination. Single point of failure if a van goes down.
5. **Extended distance from medical support.** 15-20 min to garrison TMC vs. ~5 min from LTA.
6. **Friday timeline compression.** Must begin return movement NLT 1430-1500 to allow weapons cleaning and prep before Saturday PHA. This creates a hard cutoff that may force cancellation of final lanes.

## Recommendation

**COA 2 is the fallback course of action.** It should be executed if blank fire is not approved for the LTA, or if the Commander specifically values the new environment and tactical march over additional TLP repetitions.

The primary training objective for April is maximizing TLP repetitions to prepare candidates for Phase 3. Every hour spent on movement to/from S9/S10 and on cadre preparation for new grids is an hour not spent on the stated objective. The new terrain and ruck march have training value, but they are not the priority for this training window.

If COA 2 is selected, recommend the following modifications to reduce risk:
1. Van candidates to S9/S10 (cut the ruck) to preserve training time and reduce heat risk. Conduct the ruck during a future training event when it can be the primary training objective.
2. Begin cadre leader's recon and OPORD re-gridding NLT 2 weeks prior to drill.
3. Van candidates back Friday afternoon (do not ruck return).
