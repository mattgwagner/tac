# COA 1: April TLP Training - Local Training Area with Blanks

## Classification: UNCLASSIFIED // FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY

## Overview

**Training Event:** Phase 2 TLP Focused Training (Pre-Phase 3 Preparation)
**Dates:** Thursday-Friday, April 2026 (specific drill weekend TBD)
**Location:** Local Training Area (LTA), Camp Blanding Joint Training Center, FL
**Duration:** ~30 hours continuous field operations (Thursday 1330 - Friday 1800)
**Candidates:** 24 Officer Candidates
**Cadre Required:** 4 TACs + 1 Instructor (5 total)

## Concept of the Operation

Conduct differentiated TLP-focused training from the LTA using two concurrent training tracks: a Combat Arms (CA) track emphasizing end-to-end mission execution with blank fire, and a Fundamentals (FND) track emphasizing maximum TLP planning repetitions. Both tracks operate from the same area of operations for mutual support, shared logistics, and cadre flexibility. Training includes an overnight patrol base to replicate continuous operations and prepare candidates for Phase 3 field conditions.

### Decision Point

This COA requires confirmed approval to use blank ammunition in the LTA. If blank fire is not approved for the LTA, execute COA 2 (S9/S10 Training Areas).

## Training Tracks

### Combat Arms Track (~12 Candidates)

**Cadre:** 2 Tactical/Combat Arms TACs
**Focus:** End-to-end mission execution (receive OPORD, conduct TLPs, rehearse, execute with blank fire, AAR)
**Intent:** Challenge candidates who self-identified as ready for increased complexity. Push tactical decision-making under realistic conditions. Every lane runs from OPORD to execution to AAR.

- Each lane is approximately 3-4 hours (planning through AAR)
- Estimated 4-5 full lane rotations across both training days
- Each candidate leads at minimum 1 lane as SL/TL; stronger performers get a second rep at increased complexity
- Blank ammunition used on all execution iterations
- OPFOR provided by non-leading candidates or cross-leveled from FND track
- Thursday evening limited visibility lane capitalizes on extended daylight (civil twilight 1953)

**Mission Types (drawn from existing STX OPORD library):**
- Ambush (STX 001)
- Movement to Contact (STX 002)
- Deliberate Attack (STX 014/016)
- Raid (STX 003)
- Area Reconnaissance (STX 005)

### Fundamentals Track (~12 Candidates)

**Cadre:** 2 TACs + floating Instructor
**Focus:** Maximum TLP planning repetitions with all three squads planning simultaneously
**Intent:** Address candidate-identified need for more pure reps at TLP execution for STX lanes. Prioritize quantity of planning iterations, with selective execution to validate plans against ground truth.

- 3 squads of 4 candidates, all conducting TLPs concurrently off parallel OPORDs
- Each planning cycle runs approximately 2-2.5 hours
- Estimated 6+ planning iterations per squad across both training days
- Rotate squad leaders each iteration; every candidate leads at minimum 1 full TLP cycle
- Execute approximately every other plan (abbreviated execution: leader's recon, walk-through at patrol speed, no full OPFOR required)
- Limited blank ammunition for execution iterations; dry-fire acceptable for planning-only reps
- Cadre feedback focused on: warning order quality, mission analysis, COA development, OPORD briefs, and backward planning timeline

**Mission Types:** Same library as CA track, but emphasis on clean planning products over tactical complexity.

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Heat casualties (HIGH) | Enforced water discipline, shade breaks in planning cycles, cadre monitoring, proximity to garrison medical |
| Blank fire safety (MED) | Safety brief prior to blank issue; blank fire only in designated engagement areas; safety fan established; cadre supervise all iterations |
| Candidate fatigue from continuous ops (MED) | Sleep plan in patrol base (min 4 hrs); cadre assess fatigue levels Friday PM; decision point to cut Lane 6 if needed |
| Blank approval not granted for LTA (MED) | Execute COA 2 (S9/S10); decision needed NLT 2 weeks prior to training |
| Cadre fatigue (LOW-MED) | 5 cadre rotating across 2 tracks enables rest rotation; GSA vans for chow runs; overnight shifts split |

## Advantages

1. **Maximizes training time.** Zero transport overhead. Candidates walk from company area to LTA and are training within 30 minutes of formation.
2. **Maximum TLP repetitions.** FND track achieves 6+ planning cycles; CA track achieves 4-5 full end-to-end lanes. Both tracks exceed what a single-track approach would deliver.
3. **Differentiated training addresses candidate needs.** Directly responds to AAR feedback: candidates who want more challenge get it; candidates who want more reps get them.
4. **Mutual support.** Single AO enables cadre rotation, shared logistics, cross-leveling candidates for OPFOR, and combined AAR at end of training.
5. **Logistically simple.** No new grid work required (existing LTA reference). Existing OPORD shells can be reused/modified. GSA vans support resupply, not primary transport.
6. **Overnight patrol base adds Phase 3 readiness.** Security plan, sleep plan, stand-to, priorities of work - all trained without additional time cost.
7. **Proximity to garrison.** Medical support, resupply, and emergency response all within minutes.

## Disadvantages

1. **Requires blank fire approval for LTA.** If not approved, must fall back to COA 2.
2. **Familiar terrain.** Candidates who trained here during FLX may find the environment less challenging. Mitigated by varying objectives and control measures.
3. **Limited maneuver space.** LTA may constrain lane complexity for CA track depending on available terrain. Need to verify adequate space for concurrent operations.

## Recommendation

**COA 1 is the recommended course of action**, contingent on blank fire approval for the LTA. It maximizes training time, directly addresses candidate-identified needs from the FLX AAR, and minimizes logistical friction. The time saved by eliminating transport to S9/S10 translates directly into additional TLP repetitions, which is the stated training objective.

**Decision required:** Blank fire approval for LTA, NLT 2 weeks prior to April drill.
